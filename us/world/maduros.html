Plantains (or cooking bananas) are eaten as a staple in many tropical countries around the world. Wherever plantains are eaten, they are fried. After cooking this dish, I fully understand why.

The 'center of diversity' for bananas is South-East Asia, the region where wild bananas were first cultivated. West Africans brought them to Latin America as part of the Columbian Exchange. Even today, plantains are particularly important in areas with lots of African heritage.

Other Cuban dishes we'd like to make sometime: vaca frita (fried shredded beef), pollo asado (grilled chicken), frijoles colorados (red beans).
