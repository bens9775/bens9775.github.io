Pie 48: Coconut Cream

2024-06-01

Recipe from Sally's Baking Addiction.


Taste: 6

Difficulty: 4

Vibes: 5

Suggested pairing: friendship

For most of the year, Katie Beth wanted to make a coconut cream pie. I’m not a huge fan of that wacky fruit myself, so we kept pushing it back. By June, the end of the Pie-A-Week project was near and Katie Beth resolved to make the coconut cream pie happen.

On the first Saturday of the month, she made the filling after work while I crushed some graham crackers for the crust.

The filling was a kind of cream made with lots of egg yolk, a can of coconut milk, and half-and-half. Very thick, very rich.

We made this pie to share with friends at a game night. One of those friends has a low tolerance for dairy consumption, so after I whipped cream as a topping I made sure to leave it off roughly a sixth of the pie.

You’ve seen the pictures. Whether or not your mouth is watering, you know this slice looks delicious. And yeah, it was. I thought so. Everyone thought so.

Oh, you’re still waiting? Fine, I’ll say it. I was wrong. Coconut cream pies can be great. Now get out there and vote, unless you aren’t reading this on an election day.
